\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
Psychiatric differential diagnosis from a single consultation transcript requires distinguishing overlapping symptom profiles, missing longitudinal evidence, and exclusions that are often unasked rather than explicitly negated. We introduce HiED, a hybrid, evidence-grounded multi-agent system for Chinese psychiatric differential diagnosis that separates three functions usually conflated in LLM evaluation: candidate detection, ICD-10 criterion verification, and committed primary selection. HiED combines a local Qwen3-32B-AWQ diagnostician, class-balanced FAISS/BGE-M3 case retrieval, criterion-checking agents, and deterministic threshold logic to produce a primary diagnosis, optional comorbidity, ranked differential, and auditable met/not-met/insufficient-evidence trace. On a pre-registered LingxiDiag-16K internal test split ($N=1000$), HiED--DA achieves 0.518 Top-1 and 0.802 Top-3 accuracy; retrieval improves a direct single-agent baseline from 0.466 to 0.508 (McNemar $p=4.0\times10^{-4}$). The main finding is not that verification solves diagnosis, but that it exposes a selection bottleneck: the logic layer retains the gold parent in 93.7\% of non-Others cases while confirming a median of six of fourteen active disorder entries per case. Logic re-selection, pairwise comparison, debate, deterministic fusion, selector fine-tuning, and self-consistency fail to yield a stable, causally attributable gain over Direct-Answer. External MDD-5k evaluation preserves this pattern, with 0.612 in-scope Top-1 and 0.922 F32/F41 binary accuracy. These results position HiED as an auditable research architecture for localizing failure, not as clinically validated autonomous diagnosis, and motivate transcript-matched human ceilings, structured primary selection, and evidence-acquisition policies.

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Large language models, multi-agent systems, psychiatric differential diagnosis, clinical decision support, ICD-10, auditable AI
